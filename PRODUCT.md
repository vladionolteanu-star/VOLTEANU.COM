# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

TV-show fans arriving from a link after (re)watching an episode, usually at night, on phone or desktop. They know their universe deeply and can smell lazy merch from a distance. Job to be done: find their show's storefront, browse a collection that feels curated by an insider, click out to a retailer. A second, smaller audience: partners/retailers evaluating whether volteanu.com is a serious affiliate.

## Product Purpose

volteanu.com is an aggregator of curated affiliate storefronts, one per beloved TV universe, built on the Channel3 product API. Each storefront (The Sopranos, True Detective, Breaking Bad, Stranger Things, more coming) is a themed shop with its own complete visual identity on a shared rendering engine. The root page is the front door: it routes fans to their universe and sets the tone that a person with taste built this. Success: visitors click through to a storefront and out to a retailer; the site earns commission.

## Brand Personality

Deadpan, insider, affectionate. Short declaratives. References earned, never explained. The umbrella brand (volteanu) is the landlord of a night street of shops: quieter than its tenants, but confident. Voice never sounds like marketing; it sounds like the show's world talking about objects.

## Anti-references

- Anything that reads "vibe-coded" or AI-generated: centered icon-title-subtitle stacks, gradient text, glassmorphism, hero-metric templates.
- Dropshipping-hub energy: grids of products with breathless sales copy, countdown timers, badges.
- Generic fan-merch stores (Redbubble/TeePublic look): loud, unedited, kitsch-positive.
- Identity convergence between sites: each storefront and the root page must be visually distinct worlds. Existing identities: sopranos (smoke/oxblood/neon pink, Oswald + Source Serif), true-detective (tar/sulfur/typewriter, Cormorant Garamond + Special Elite + Lora), breaking-bad (manila auction catalog, Saira Stencil One + IBM Plex), stranger-things (indigo night/signal red/cream flyers, Fraunces + Literata + Share Tech Mono). New surfaces reuse none of these palettes or font stacks.

## Design Principles

- Curation is the product: fewer, better objects; a 7-item honest shelf beats a 15-item padded one. Copy and layout must communicate editing, not abundance.
- Rooms of the world, not e-commerce categories: every surface is framed as a place in the fiction (a shrine, a case file, an aisle), never as "Apparel / Accessories".
- The engine is shared, the identity never is: structural quality comes from one renderer; personality comes from per-site tokens and copy. The root page gets its own committed identity too.
- Every word earns its place, in-voice: product notes and interface copy sound like the world of the show. No em dashes anywhere.
- Monetization is stated plainly, once, in the footer, in-voice. Never hidden, never shouted.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal WCAG target declared; practical bar: honest contrast on dark grounds, visible focus states on every interactive element, prefers-reduced-motion respected (already standard in the shared renderer), alt text present, layouts usable at 360px phone width.
